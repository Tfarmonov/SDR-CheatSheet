The golden rule

Your only job is to make the cost of doing nothing feel more uncomfortable than spending 20 minutes with an AE. Never ask for more than the meeting.

Official objection navigator
"We already have a system."
Acknowledge it — then ask where the friction still lives. Most systems miss pieces. PlanetBids was built end-to-end for public procurement.
↳ Are there parts of your current system that feel clunky or time-consuming?
"We need finance/ERP integration."
PlanetBids is purpose-built for procurement. Many clients run it alongside their ERP — it handles procurement better than any all-in-one tool.
↳ If it reduced procurement time/cost, would it be worth looking at side-by-side?
"Budget is tight."
That's exactly why agencies evaluate us — 55–88% admin reduction often offsets the cost. Fewer staff hours on manual work = real savings.
↳ What does a manual bid cycle cost your team in hours today?
"We just went through an implementation."
Timing matters. Stay in touch, provide value. PlanetBids implements fast — when they're ready to evaluate again, be first.
↳ When does your current contract expire? I'd love to reconnect at the right time.

Procurement solicitation types
RFP
Request for Proposal — agency describes a problem, vendors propose a solution + price. Open-ended; evaluates approach and cost. Used when the solution isn't fully defined.
ITB
Invitation to Bid — agency knows exactly what it wants, vendors submit a price. Lowest qualified bid wins. Used for construction, supplies, equipment.
RFQ
Request for Quote — simpler, often informal pricing request for smaller purchases. Vendors just submit a price quote.
RFSQ
Request for Supplier Qualifications — pre-screening vendors before any contract. Vendors submit credentials to get on an approved list.
Key terms
Bid protest
A formal challenge filed by a vendor who believes a procurement wasn't fair. Without an airtight audit trail, agencies are exposed — and it's public record.
Audit trail
A complete, time-stamped record of every action during procurement. PlanetBids creates this automatically.
eProcurement
Using software to manage the full solicitation lifecycle online instead of paper and email.
AEP
Achievement of Excellence in Procurement — national award for innovation and professionalism. A credibility signal with prospects.

What is SPIN selling?

SPIN is a research-backed framework for complex B2B sales. Instead of pitching features, you guide the prospect through a conversation that makes them feel the pain themselves — so the meeting with the AE becomes their idea, not your ask.

Discovery questions that open deals

Official PlanetBids discovery framework — use in sequence.

Current State
How does your team manage the bid process today?
Are you using a platform — or is it largely manual?
Pain Discovery
Where does the most time get lost in a typical procurement cycle?
How do you currently handle vendor communication during a bid?
Compliance & Risk
How confident are you in your current audit trail?
Have you had to deal with a protest or a compliance issue recently?
Future State
If you could fix one thing about your procurement process, what would it be?
What would "great" look like for your team 12 months from now?
S — Situation questions

Establish context. Don't overdo it — too many feel like an interrogation.

"How does your team currently manage the procurement process — is everything online, or still partially manual?"
"What system are you using today to post bids and manage vendor submissions?"
"How many solicitations does your agency run in a typical year?"
"How many people are on your procurement team right now?"
P — Problem questions

Uncover pain. Let them say it — not you.

"How difficult is it to track vendor certifications and insurance renewals?"
"When you need to pull documentation for a public records request or audit, how long does that take?"
"Are you happy with the number of vendor responses on bids, or do the same few vendors keep showing up?"
"Has your team ever had to deal with a bid protest? How did that go?"
I — Implication questions

Amplify the pain. Connect problems to bigger consequences.

"If a vendor's insurance lapsed and no one caught it, what would that mean for an active contract or a public records request?"
"If you're getting 3–4 responses per bid instead of 10–12, how much is that affecting pricing — and how does that look to council?"
"If a bid protest came in tomorrow and you needed a full audit trail in 48 hours, how confident would you be?"
"When staff spends hours on manual tracking instead of strategic work, what does that mean for capacity if someone retires?"
N — Need-payoff questions

Get them to describe the solution themselves. Then bridge to the AE meeting.

"If a system automatically tracked every cert expiration and sent your team an alert before anything lapsed — how much time would that save?"
"If you could walk into any audit knowing every step was already documented — how much stress would that take off your team?"
"If your vendor response rate doubled — from 4 to 10 — how would that change conversations with council?"
SPIN-to-CTA bridge
"That's exactly what agencies like [peer city] are experiencing after switching to PlanetBids. I don't want to oversell it on a cold call — but I think it's worth 20 minutes with one of our Account Executives who works specifically with agencies your size. Would [Day] or [Day] work?"
